# Visual workflow

## Establish the direction

Write one sentence in the user's own language. Preserve unusual references and tensions instead of translating them into a familiar theme name.

Identify:

- the emotional tone and relevant cultural or artistic references;
- how space, scale, rhythm, light, and color should behave;
- the tactile qualities the interface should suggest;
- whether identity depends on a subject or can emerge from abstract form and texture;
- where native content needs visual quiet;
- how much motion supports the idea without distracting from work.

Do not map these observations onto a preset category. If the request is ambiguous, explore a few interpretations internally and ask only when the alternatives would materially change the result.

## Synthesize the system

Define the art direction before opening the manifest. Make composition, palette, surfaces, typography, imagery, and motion express the same idea. Translate the direction into fields only afterward.

## Prepare artwork

For supplied artwork, inspect it before choosing colors or crop. Preserve the intended subject and create a quiet content zone. Remove embedded text, watermarks, fake controls, and irrelevant borders only when authorized.

For generated artwork, specify the scene or subject, material and lighting, palette, wide desktop composition, quiet content zone, and negative constraints: no words, logo, UI, watermark, or frame.

Use procedural artwork when identity can emerge from abstract shape, texture, rhythm, light, and color. Use image artwork when it depends on a specific subject, place, object, or illustration.

## Build for reading

Choose functional background, surface, text, muted, and accent colors. Sample from the artwork, then normalize for UI use. Maintain at least 4.5:1 contrast for normal text against background and surface.

Derive opacity, blur, border, shadow, radius, grain, and glow from the same tactile world. Avoid contradictory effects unless the tension is intentional.

Review at least home and task states when a preview exists. Fix interaction, readability, composer clarity, content collision, hierarchy, and only then decorative polish.

## Rights

Use user-owned, generated, licensed, or public-domain artwork. Record attribution when required. For public-domain art reinterpretations, record the original artists or works and create a new composition; remember that a modern scan or photograph of an old work may carry separate rights. Do not assume that a public image, celebrity photo, character, logo, or brand asset can be redistributed.
